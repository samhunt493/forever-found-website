In the UK, microchipping is a legal requirement for dogs and is increasingly common for cats and other pets. A microchip is a tiny, passive device (not a tracker or GPS) that is inserted just under your pet’s skin. It contains a unique identification number which is linked to your contact details in a secure national database.

If your pet becomes lost or is picked up by a vet, rescue centre, or animal warden, a quick scan of the chip can bring up your registered details so they can contact you directly. It’s a reliable way to confirm ownership and ensure your pet can get home safely, even if their collar or tag is lost. Microchipping is safe, permanent, and one of the most effective ways to protect your pet.
